Resistance to elements is a major concern, which is why teak is used. Teak is unquestionably the most popular wood for outdoor furniture. It is extremely strong and does not warp, crack, or become brittle like many other types of wood. It has a high natural oil content, making it highly resistant to rot, decay, and insect damage. Its gorgeous honeybrown appearance can be kept with very little oiling. The upholstery on outdoor furniture retains its structural integrity and appearance over many seasons and in a variety of weather conditions.
